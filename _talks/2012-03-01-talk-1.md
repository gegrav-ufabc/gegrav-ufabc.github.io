Analog models of gravity provide a laboratory setting to investigate curved space phenomena.
In this context, linear magnetoelectric materials offer interesting possibilities for modeling such
analog geometries. Here, general conditions under which a light ray enters a one-way (trapped)
region, bounded by an analog event horizon, are identified. The results, thereby, establish linear
magnetoelectric materials as a new platform for analog black hole physics.
